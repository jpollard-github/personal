# LEAD-GENERATION-TODO

Purpose:

This is Jason's business-growth operating document for finding first consulting clients.

It is not a publishing backlog, not a persona-framework roadmap, and not a CRM build plan.

Use it to run small, human outreach experiments, track what creates real conversations, and improve the website where that helps trust and conversion.

## Core Principle

Do not buy lists, spam people, blast generic messages, or automate relationship-building out of existence.

The strategy is:

- 2-3 high-quality personalized outreach messages per week
- small experiments across multiple channels
- lead-source tracking
- follow-up discipline
- useful public content
- repeatable but human outreach

Automate the repetitive parts, not the real relationship-building.

## Outreach Messages

### LinkedIn connection request

Use this text:

Hi! I'm a local software developer in the Triad. I build small internal tools and workflow automations for businesses that have outgrown spreadsheets and manual processes. I enjoy connecting with other local business owners and operations leaders.

### First email

Subject: Quick question about internal workflows

Hi,

I’m Jason, a software developer here in the Triad.

I help small and mid-sized businesses replace repetitive spreadsheet, email, and manual processes with simple internal web tools.

I came across your business and wondered if there are any internal tasks your team wishes were easier—things like request tracking, approvals, reporting, scheduling, or data entry.

If so, I'd be happy to spend 20–30 minutes learning about the problem. Even if we decide not to work together, I'm glad to share ideas that might save your team time.

You can also see some of my work and background here:

https://arcadeghosts.org/work-with-me

Thanks for your time, and I wish you continued success.

Jason

### Short follow-up

Subject: Re: Quick question about internal workflows

Hi,

Just wanted to follow up once in case this got buried.

If you have any spreadsheet, email, reporting, intake, or workflow pain points, I’d be happy to talk through whether a small internal tool could help.

No pressure either way.

Thanks,
Jason

## ChatGPT Prompts For Prospect Research

### Build a 100-prospect list

Focus on businesses likely to have spreadsheet, email, intake, reporting, scheduling, or workflow pain. Please organize the list into 5 categories of 20 prospects each: manufacturers/operations-heavy businesses, medical/clinic/wellness practices, accounting/insurance/law/professional services, property management/real estate/construction/trades, and nonprofits/community organizations. For each prospect, include company name, website, likely decision-maker role, why they might have operational pain, and where to look for contact info. Do not invent personal email addresses.

### Find 10 prospects in one category

Find 10 potential prospects in the Triad region for [CATEGORY]. Prioritize businesses that appear established, local or regional, and likely to rely on manual internal processes. For each, provide website, LinkedIn or company page if available, likely contact role, and a short reason they may be a fit. Do not invent private contact details.

### Personalize outreach

### Create LinkedIn connection notes

Given this prospect list: [PASTE COMPANIES], write short LinkedIn connection requests for owners, operations managers, office managers, practice managers, or executive directors. Keep each under LinkedIn limits, local, useful, and not pushy.
