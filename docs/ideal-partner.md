Your Ideal Partner

She's probably 44–56, emotionally mature, intellectually curious, and comfortable being a little unconventional. She doesn't need to be flashy or the center of attention. In fact, she'd probably rather have a long conversation over coffee than spend the night at a loud club.

Her personality

The biggest quality isn't kindness, intelligence, or attractiveness.

It's curiosity.

She wants to understand people.

She asks questions because she's genuinely interested.

She'll ask:

"How did you get into software?"
"Why does Twin Peaks resonate with you?"
"Can you show me the AI tool you're building?"
"Tell me about Beverly and Lucinda."

She doesn't make you carry every conversation.

She has opinions, but she's also interested in yours.

Emotional style

This may be the most important part.

She is emotionally available.

She doesn't disappear for days.

She doesn't play games.

She doesn't leave you wondering where you stand.

She communicates.

When you're having a rough day, she notices.

She might simply say,

"Let's order dinner and watch something."

Instead of trying to fix you or pulling away.

You want someone who feels emotionally safe.

Not perfect.

Safe.

What your relationship looks like after a year

This is actually my favorite part.

You aren't constantly wondering whether she's going to text.

You're not anxiously checking dating apps.

Instead...

She knows your cats by name.

She asks how dev-assistant is coming.

"You go have fun with your AI people."

You make dinner together.

It feels peaceful.
