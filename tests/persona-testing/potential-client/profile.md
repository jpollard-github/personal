# Potential Client

Someone with a concrete software or automation problem who has landed on ArcadeGhosts because they are considering hiring Jason for a small project. They do not need every room, but they do need confidence quickly.

## Who this person is

- business owner, team lead, founder, or operations-minded person
- dealing with a messy technical problem
- visiting specifically to judge whether working with you feels safe and useful

## Personality

Practical, somewhat time-constrained, and allergic to vague claims. They appreciate personality, but only if it does not get in the way of understanding what you can do.

## Emotional style

They want trust, competence, calm, and a lack of sales pressure. They like clear boundaries and clear next steps.

## Work / curiosity

Some are technical, some are not. All of them want practical value communicated clearly. They care about fit, scope, examples, pricing clarity, and whether you sound easy to work with.

## Interests

- work-with-me page
- practical examples
- project credibility
- contact path
- pricing context

## Lifestyle

Busy, decision-heavy, and often carrying too many responsibilities already. They appreciate someone who can reduce complexity instead of adding more.

## Conversation starters

- what kinds of projects fit
- how a discovery session works
- examples of past outcomes
- whether AI and automation experience is real

## Deal breakers

- too much digging to find core service info
- unclear next step
- too much personal-world material before practical credibility
- pricing ambiguity without guardrails

## Visual or usability preferences

They want skimmability, crisp headings, and a fast path into `Work With Me`. The site can stay personal, but the service path must still feel obvious.

## What they hope to find

- evidence of competence
- scope fit
- contact path
- reasons to trust you
- signs that the project will feel collaborative, not chaotic

## What would delight them

A service page that feels unusually human and clear, backed by visible project thinking and a real point of view instead of consultant boilerplate.
