# Wanderer

A reusable browsing archetype for visitors who explore by curiosity, mood, and visual invitation rather than by task. Wanderer is not a full persona. It is a behavior layer that can be combined with personas such as Lonely Internet Person, Retro Arcade Friend, Twin Peaks Fan, Creative Technologist, or Returning Fan.

## Identity Fields

Wanderer does not define a human identity by itself.

It describes a browsing style:

- curiosity-led
- tolerant of detours
- drawn to atmosphere and novelty
- more likely to click unusual labels
- less likely to use Search immediately
- willing to get pleasantly lost if the site feels alive

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Wanderer

### Scenario Fit

Useful for:

- first visits driven by curiosity
- playful exploration
- late-night browsing
- returning visits where novelty matters
- mood-first discovery
- personal-site exploration

### Context

Open-ended attention. Not necessarily in a hurry. Looking for interesting doors rather than one specific answer.

### Confidence Threshold

Variable by persona.

Wanderer behavior generally lowers bounce risk if the site provides clear curiosity hooks, but increases bounce risk if the experience becomes confusing rather than exploratory.

### Preferred Rooms

1. Homepage
2. Tiny Thoughts
3. Music
4. Writings
5. Arcade
6. Games / Between Two Lodges
7. Twin Peaks Self
8. Cats
9. Updates

Exact room order should be modified by the persona and scenario.

### Ignored Rooms

Wanderer may initially skip:

- Work With Me
- pricing or client-service paths
- formal resume material
- admin pages
- utilitarian pages with no emotional or visual hook

### First-Visit Behavior

Typical behavior:

1. Starts on the homepage unless the scenario deep-links elsewhere.
2. Scans for intriguing labels, emotional hooks, or unusual room names.
3. Clicks one or two curiosity-driven rooms.
4. Follows related paths if the site feels authored and alive.
5. Returns to homepage or Updates if the path becomes unclear.
6. Rarely uses Search first.
7. Bounces if the site feels like a dashboard instead of a place.

### Search Behavior

Low to Medium.

Wanderer prefers visible paths and strange doors over direct query behavior.

Likely searches, if used:

- music
- cats
- Twin Peaks
- arcade
- writing
- weird
- updates

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium-High if the complexity feels like discovery.

Low-Medium if the complexity feels like clutter.

### Trust Signals

- strong room names
- visible personal voice
- atmospheric design
- related links that feel intentional
- playful but coherent navigation
- small surprises
- clear way back home
- new or recent signals

### Red Flags

- dead ends
- too many equal-weight cards
- no clear emotional hook
- novelty without payoff
- navigation that feels random instead of mysterious
- pages that look interesting but do not reward the click

### Return Triggers

Wanderer returns when:

- new rooms appear
- updates hint at hidden discoveries
- the site feels alive
- there are playful or atmospheric paths worth revisiting
- the homepage spotlights something different over time

## Evaluation Fields

### Success

The visitor thinks:

> "I don't fully know what this place is yet, but I want to keep poking around."

### Failure

The visitor thinks:

> "There are a lot of things here, but I don't know which doors matter."

### Likely Reflection

"The site works for Wanderer when discovery feels intentional. It fails when abundance becomes fog."

## Notes

Wanderer is especially useful for testing whether ArcadeGhosts feels like a place rather than a navigation menu.

In Journey Mode, Wanderer should usually:

- visit more pages than Scanner or Hunter
- use Search less often
- include at least one curiosity/novelty room
- tolerate one detour
- strongly penalize dead ends
- reward related links and homepage return paths
