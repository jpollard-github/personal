# Reader

A pure browsing archetype for visitors who are willing to read deeply when the writing path feels calm, coherent, and worth the attention. Reader is not a full persona; it is a reusable behavior layer.

## Identity Fields

Reader does not define a human identity by itself.

It describes a browsing style:

- patient with prose
- sensitive to voice
- drawn to essays and reflective pages
- tolerant of length when structure is good
- less interested in dashboards or noisy navigation

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Reader

### Scenario Fit

Useful for:

- essay discovery
- About page evaluation
- personal-site resonance
- thoughtful first visits
- RSS/subscriber behavior
- emotionally open contexts

### Context

Quiet attention. Willing to linger if the site earns it.

### Confidence Threshold

Variable by persona, but a strong opening lowers the threshold quickly.

### Preferred Rooms

Reader prefers:

- Writings
- About
- Tiny Thoughts
- individual essays
- reflective cat/memory pages
- update pages with clear excerpts

### Ignored Rooms

Reader often skips:

- admin pages
- purely technical controls
- dense lists without narrative context
- games unless they have strong writing
- visual pages with little text

### First-Visit Behavior

- looks for a calm reading path
- opens one meaningful page
- reads past the first screen if the voice is strong
- follows related links when they feel editorially chosen
- may subscribe if there is a feed

### Search Behavior

Low-Medium.

Reader prefers curated paths, but may search for themes.

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium.

High tolerance for depth, low tolerance for clutter.

### Trust Signals

- strong openings
- clear authorial voice
- emotional specificity
- related pieces
- quiet layout
- RSS or archive path

### Red Flags

- cleverness without warmth
- fragments without depth
- noisy homepage
- technical copy before human context
- no obvious writing path

### Return Triggers

Reader returns when new essays, Tiny Thoughts, or meaningful updates appear.

## Evaluation Fields

### Success

The site gives Reader a clear, rewarding path into voice and meaning.

### Failure

The site has writing, but it is buried or framed as content instead of invitation.

### Likely Reflection

"I stayed because the site slowed down enough to let the voice come through."

## Notes

Reader is important for evaluating essays, About, emotional resonance, and whether longer content has enough room to breathe.
