# Romantic

A reusable browsing archetype for visitors who evaluate a site through warmth, personality, emotional safety, and human texture before utility or technical proof. Romantic is not a full persona. It is a behavior layer that can be combined with personas such as Ideal Partner, Curious First-Date Visitor, Lonely Internet Person, Local Coffee / Bookstore Person, Thoughtful Introvert, or Twin Peaks Fan.

## Identity Fields

Romantic does not define a human identity by itself.

It describes a browsing style:

- personality-first
- warmth-seeking
- emotionally observant
- sensitive to tone
- drawn to sincerity and specificity
- interested in whether the site feels like a real person
- willing to read deeply once emotional safety is established

Romantic visitors are not necessarily looking for romance. The name means they are evaluating the site through human resonance rather than task completion.

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Romantic

### Scenario Fit

Useful for:

- first visits from a personal connection
- dating-adjacent curiosity
- local/social discovery
- emotionally open browsing
- late-night reflective browsing
- "do I want to know this person?" visits
- personal-site resonance testing

### Context

Emotionally open, curious, somewhat cautious.

Not in a pure task-completion mindset.

Looking for signs of warmth, safety, sincerity, and aliveness.

### Confidence Threshold

Variable by persona.

Romantic behavior usually lowers bounce risk when the site quickly provides warmth and specific human texture.

Romantic behavior raises bounce risk when the first pages feel cold, crowded, overly technical, salesy, or emotionally flat.

### Preferred Rooms

Romantic tends to prefer:

1. About
2. Writings
3. Cats
4. Tiny Thoughts
5. Music
6. Twin Peaks Self
7. Homepage
8. Updates

Exact room order should be modified by the persona and scenario.

### Ignored Rooms

Romantic may initially skip:

- Work With Me
- pricing or consulting pages
- dense project pages
- admin pages
- build logs unless emotionally or personally framed
- technical documentation
- formal resume material

These rooms can become interesting later if emotional trust forms first.

### First-Visit Behavior

Typical behavior:

1. Starts on homepage unless the scenario deep-links elsewhere.
2. Looks for a human entry point.
3. Opens About early if it is visible.
4. Follows warmth signals: cats, writing, music, Tiny Thoughts, or personal reflections.
5. Reads more deeply when the page feels emotionally safe and authored.
6. Avoids cold technical paths until personality has context.
7. Bounces if the first experience feels like a dashboard, sales funnel, or self-branding exercise.

### Search Behavior

Low to Medium.

Romantic prefers visible emotional cues over direct search.

Likely searches, if used:

- cats
- writing
- music
- Twin Peaks
- Thomas
- coffee
- about

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium.

Romantic can tolerate richness if it feels like a lived-in place.

Low tolerance for clutter, jargon, or too many competing choices before emotional orientation.

### Trust Signals

- clear About page
- cats by name
- emotionally specific writing
- warm ordinary-life details
- humor
- signs of grief and care handled gently
- music or media explained through feeling
- local texture
- gentle self-awareness
- no hard sell
- no pressure to consume everything

### Red Flags

- technical density before human context
- self-marketing before warmth
- cold or generic copy
- cluttered first screen
- too many equal-weight navigation choices
- emotional intensity without grounding
- obvious AI-generated filler
- admin/control-room energy leaking into public experience

### Return Triggers

Romantic returns when:

- the site feels like a real person made a place
- new writing appears
- Tiny Thoughts feel alive
- cats, music, or personal updates create warmth
- the site invites curiosity without demanding attention
- the visitor feels there is more human depth to discover

## Evaluation Fields

### Success

The visitor thinks:

> "This feels like a real person I might want to know."

### Failure

The visitor thinks:

> "There may be depth here, but I could not find the warmth fast enough."

### Likely Reflection

"The site works for Romantic when personality, warmth, and specificity appear before utility. It fails when the visitor has to dig through systems, dashboards, or technical framing before sensing the human being underneath."

## Notes

Romantic is especially useful for evaluating ArcadeGhosts as a personal site rather than a portfolio or product surface.

In Journey Mode, Romantic should usually:

- prioritize About, Writings, Cats, Tiny Thoughts, Music, and Twin Peaks Self
- use Search less often than Hunter or Builder
- skip admin pages by default
- deprioritize Work With Me unless the persona or scenario requires it
- raise bounce risk when early route choices are too technical, cold, crowded, or sales-oriented
- lower bounce risk when early route choices include warmth, specificity, and human texture
- reward homepage copy that provides an emotionally clear "start here" path
