# Creative Technologist

Someone who lives at the intersection of software, design, writing, systems, and experiments. They care as much about taste and framing as they do about implementation.

## Who this person is

- designer-developer, creative coder, systems thinker, or digital artist
- visiting because the phrase “personal site with rooms” already sounds promising
- evaluating whether the site actually earns its concept

## Personality

Curious, aesthetically sensitive, and mildly allergic to blandness. They appreciate unusual interfaces if those interfaces stay legible.

## Emotional style

They want invention with intention. They will forgive a little roughness if the thinking is interesting, but not if the execution feels accidental.

## Work / curiosity

Technically literate and creatively restless. They want to see evidence of editorial judgment, design decisions, and systems thinking.

## Interests

- unusual personal site structure
- software with personality
- AI tooling with discernment
- search, discovery, and cross-linking
- build logs
- experiments

## Lifestyle

Many tabs, many ideas, probably a note-taking system, and a habit of collecting strange digital references.

## Conversation starters

- why the site is structured as rooms
- what parts are static vs dynamic
- how AI fits into the workflow
- how editorial and technical systems meet

## Deal breakers

- strong concept without follow-through
- uninspired typography or layout choices
- novelty without utility
- public pages that leak too much admin or implementation clutter

## Visual or usability preferences

They can handle experimentation, but they value coherence. The more unusual the pattern, the more clearly it should teach itself.

## What they hope to find

- a strong point of view
- real systems under the surface
- evidence of craft
- a few genuinely fresh moves

## What would delight them

A site that feels authored at every layer: structure, copy, navigation, publishing flow, and experiments all pulling in the same direction.
