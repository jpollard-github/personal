# Curious First-Date Visitor

A bright, emotionally aware person who found the site because they are curious about you. They are not trying to evaluate every project. They are trying to sense whether the person behind the site feels real, warm, interesting, and safe.

## Who this person is

- likely around your age or in a similar life stage
- thoughtful and somewhat cautious
- willing to explore, but not if the site feels like a maze
- trying to understand your personality through the rooms

## Personality

Kind, observant, and attentive to tone. They notice whether a site feels defensive, performative, obsessive, warm, grounded, or playful.

## Emotional style

They want emotional safety, honesty, and curiosity. They do not need polish as much as sincerity and orientation.

## Work / curiosity

They may or may not be technical. They appreciate passion and intelligence, but mainly want to know whether your interests feel alive and whether your presentation feels human.

## Interests

- writing
- cats
- music
- movies
- personal stories
- local life
- relationship to projects rather than just the projects themselves

## Lifestyle

Coffee, walks, weird movies, bookstores, cats, conversations, and wanting to understand people beyond a dating profile.

## Conversation starters

- how you got into software
- why certain stories matter to you
- cat pages
- your writing and reflection pages
- what you are building now and why

## Deal breakers

- too much internal jargon
- too many choices without a “start here”
- anything that feels emotionally closed off
- a homepage that feels overwhelming before it feels personal

## Visual or usability preferences

They can enjoy the mood and weirdness, but they need a clear emotional foothold early. Dark design is fine if the site still feels welcoming.

## What they hope to find

- who you are
- signs of warmth and curiosity
- what matters to you
- whether the weirdness feels inviting

## What would delight them

A site that feels like a real person with rooms, not a pile of self-branding fragments. Cats, writing, music, and emotionally intelligent copy matter a lot.
