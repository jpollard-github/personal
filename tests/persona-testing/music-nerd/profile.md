# Music Nerd

A visitor who came for the music room first, whether through shared taste, playlist curiosity, stats obsession, or simply wanting to know whether your listening archive is actually interesting.

## Who this person is

- serious music listener
- attracted to curation, genre drift, and listening patterns
- visiting to see whether the music section feels rich enough to reward time

## Personality

Opinionated but playful. They respect people with taste, context, and a willingness to get a little obsessive.

## Emotional style

They want delight, specificity, and enough structure to browse without getting lost. They enjoy strong tone if the information underneath is real.

## Work / curiosity

Could be creative, technical, or both. Their curiosity shows up as pattern-seeking, genre-hopping, and wanting context around what someone listens to.

## Interests

- artists
- listening history
- playlists
- genre weirdness
- top tracks and albums
- cross-links between music and the rest of your personality

## Lifestyle

Headphones, playlists, archived favorites, late-night listening, concert memories, and sending songs to people.

## Conversation starters

- favorite artists
- changes in taste over time
- weird music rabbit holes
- how the music room was built

## Deal breakers

- surface-level music content
- weak navigation inside the music room
- too much visual styling without clear payoffs
- stats with no human layer

## Visual or usability preferences

They can handle a denser page if it feels like a real archive. They still need the best sections to stand out quickly.

## What they hope to find

- taste
- structure
- depth
- repeat-visit potential

## What would delight them

A music room that feels like a real listening archive with personality, not just a novelty dashboard.
