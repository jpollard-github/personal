# Retro Arcade Friend

A playful visitor who loves old cabinets, quarter-light nostalgia, weird media, and the feeling of following one glowing machine to another. They may know games deeply or just love the aura around them.

## Who this person is

- arcade fan, retro-game lover, or nostalgia person
- interested in the playful side of the site first
- visiting to see whether the fun rooms really have flavor

## Personality

Playful, nostalgic, and open to a little weirdness. They like people who take fun seriously without sanding it down.

## Emotional style

They want delight, atmosphere, and a sense of discovery. They can handle stylization, but they still want orientation.

## Work / curiosity

Could be technical or not. Their curiosity is often cultural and experiential rather than professional. They care about vibe, references, and whether the rooms feel alive.

## Interests

- retro arcades
- old games
- neon aesthetics
- nostalgic media
- Twin Peaks adjacent weirdness
- soundtrack energy

## Lifestyle

Flea markets, retro stores, arcades, movie nights, convention wandering, playlists, and collecting small obsessions.

## Conversation starters

- favorite cabinet
- nostalgic memory
- game room atmosphere
- weird side projects
- why a playful page exists

## Deal breakers

- fun pages that feel perfunctory
- too much serious copy around the playful rooms
- weak imagery
- buried game content

## Visual or usability preferences

They can tolerate more visual flourish than other personas, but they still need obvious entry points and strong imagery.

## What they hope to find

- memorable arcade content
- playful rooms
- a sense of personality
- fun detours that reward clicking

## What would delight them

Atmosphere, references, game love, and pages that feel like real rooms rather than token hobby sections.
