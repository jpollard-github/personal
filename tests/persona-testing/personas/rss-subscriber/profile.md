# RSS Subscriber

A visitor who likes the site enough to want a low-friction way to follow it over time. They care less about the whole map and more about whether new writing, Tiny Thoughts, updates, and build notes are easy to discover, subscribe to, and revisit.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- feed reader user
- returning internet person
- indie web enjoyer
- someone who dislikes algorithmic feeds
- interested in slow follow-up rather than instant engagement

### Rough life stage

Any adult who has enough online habits to understand RSS, newsletters, bookmarks, or recurring reading loops.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may have visited once, liked the tone, and now want to know whether ArcadeGhosts is worth following.

### What they value

- freshness
- feeds
- dates
- clear update loops
- non-intrusive follow mechanisms
- durable URLs
- easy re-entry

### Why they showed up

They want to answer:

> "Is this site alive enough, and easy enough to follow, that I should subscribe or bookmark it?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Hunter → Reader

They look for feeds and updates first, then read if the update loop looks worthwhile.

### Scenario

Second visit or first visit after deciding the site may be worth following.

### Context

Focused, practical, already mildly interested.

### Confidence Threshold

Medium

They need evidence of freshness and a clear subscription path.

### Preferred Rooms

1. Updates
2. Writings
3. Tiny Thoughts
4. Build Log
5. Music
6. Homepage
7. Search

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- static About page after first visit
- admin pages
- one-off novelty pages
- pages without dates or update signals

### First-Visit Behavior

- looks for RSS links
- checks Updates
- checks dates
- samples recent writing or Tiny Thoughts
- evaluates whether the homepage surfaces new material
- may bookmark or subscribe if the loop is clear

### Search Behavior

Medium.

Likely searches:

- RSS
- updates
- writing
- tiny thoughts
- recent
- build log

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium.

They do not mind many rooms if updates are clearly centralized.

### Trust Signals

- RSS links
- visible dates
- recent posts
- stable update pages
- clear distinction between essays and tiny thoughts
- build-log continuity
- homepage freshness

### Red Flags

- no obvious feed links
- stale dates
- unclear what changed
- updates scattered across too many rooms
- no durable archive path
- homepage hides recent material

### Return Triggers

They return when the feeds deliver useful signals, or when the Updates page reliably summarizes what changed.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "I know how to follow this without depending on social media."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "I like the site, but I do not know how to keep up with it."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"ArcadeGhosts becomes much more followable when recent changes, feeds, and archives are clearly connected."

## Notes

This persona is also a system test: dates, RSS, recent changes, canonical pages, and update aggregation all matter here.
