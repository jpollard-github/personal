# Reading Enthusiast

A thoughtful reader who loves essays, independent blogs, libraries, bookstores, and internet spaces with an actual voice. They may arrive through a link, a recommendation, an RSS feed, or curiosity after seeing one of Jason's smaller updates.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- reader
- writer
- essay enjoyer
- independent blog person
- someone who values voice and emotional intelligence

### Rough life stage

Any adult, likely someone who has enough life experience to value reflective writing over content churn.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may not know Jason personally. The writing is their first handshake.

### What they value

- voice
- emotional honesty
- clarity
- memory
- specific details
- quiet humor
- a reason to return

### Why they showed up

They want to answer:

> "Is the writing here worth lingering with?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Reader

They are willing to read deeply if the opening signal is strong.

### Scenario

First visit focused on writing, essays, and whether the author has a voice worth following.

### Context

Quiet attention. Possibly late evening or weekend browsing.

### Confidence Threshold

Low-Medium

A strong essay opening wins them quickly, but generic copy loses them fast.

### Preferred Rooms

1. Writings
2. Individual essays
3. About
4. Tiny Thoughts
5. Updates
6. Cats
7. Music

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- Work With Me
- admin pages
- technical project details
- games unless linked to memory or essay voice

### First-Visit Behavior

- looks for Writings or About
- reads the first essay that feels emotionally specific
- checks whether short thoughts point toward longer depth
- notices if the homepage gives too many competing options
- may subscribe if the writing room feels alive

### Search Behavior

Low-Medium.

They prefer curated paths but may search for topics.

Likely searches:

- writing
- grief
- cats
- memory
- technology
- identity

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium.

They tolerate rich archives if the reading path is calm.

### Trust Signals

- strong essay titles
- emotionally concrete openings
- restraint
- personal details without melodrama
- clear writing room purpose
- RSS or return path
- related essays that make sense

### Red Flags

- too much technical framing before writing
- fragmented notes with no deeper room behind them
- cleverness without warmth
- noisy homepage
- essays buried too deeply

### Return Triggers

They return for new essays, RSS, Tiny Thoughts that hint at deeper writing, and updates that show ongoing reflection.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "This person has a real voice. I want to read another piece."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "The site has personality, but I did not find a clear reading path."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"The writing is strongest when the site slows down and lets the voice carry the room."

## Notes

This persona should reward calm reading paths, strong essay openings, RSS, and related-content links.
