# Your Ideal Partner

She is emotionally mature, intellectually curious, warm, and comfortable being a little unconventional. She is not looking for endless noise or performative excitement. She is looking for a real person with depth, steadiness, humor, and a life that feels genuinely lived.

## Identity Fields

### Who she is

She is likely somewhere in midlife, emotionally available, and established enough to know what kind of energy she wants around her. She does not need someone flashy. She wants someone thoughtful, interesting, grounded, and real.

### Values

She values curiosity, emotional safety, warmth, consistency, intelligence without arrogance, and a life that feels textured rather than optimized to impress strangers.

### Emotional needs

She wants calm more than chaos, depth more than hype, and sincerity more than performance. She likes feeling that there is a real human behind the website.

## Behavior Fields

### Default archetype

Romantic Reader.

She looks for personality before competence, but once she feels safe she is willing to read and linger.

### Scenario

First visit after seeing a business card, a dating profile mention, a meetup reference, or a shared link.

### Context

Evening curiosity. She has enough time to browse and is emotionally open, but still somewhat cautious.

### Confidence threshold

Low.

If the site feels warm, real, and emotionally coherent fairly quickly, she is willing to trust it and keep going.

### Preferred rooms

About
Writings
Cats
Music
Twin Peaks Self
Tiny Thoughts
Homepage

### Ignored rooms

Admin pages.

She is unlikely to care about backend/editorial tools at all.

She may also skip or lightly skim:

- Work With Me
- Build Log
- deeply technical project/admin details unless they are framed in a personal way

### First-visit behavior

She probably starts on the homepage and quickly asks:

- does this feel like a real person?
- does this site have warmth?
- is there actual depth here?

If the homepage feels inviting, she likely goes to:

1. About
2. Writings
3. Cats or Tiny Thoughts
4. Music or Twin Peaks Self

She is unlikely to use Search first.

She will scroll if the page feels calm and authored.

She may bounce if the first screen feels too dense, too technical, or emotionally cold.

### Trust signals

What makes her trust Jason:

- reflective writing
- warmth
- cat stories and photographs
- evidence of curiosity
- emotionally grounded self-expression
- specific tastes and references
- a sense that the site was made with care, not just assembled

### Red flags

What makes her hesitate:

- too much density too early
- too much technical detail before personality
- too much self-marketing
- anything that feels performative or inauthentic
- admin/control-room energy leaking into the public feeling of the site

### Return triggers

What makes her come back:

- finding one or two pieces of writing that feel intimate or thoughtful
- feeling like the site contains a whole person, not just a resume
- seeing recurring small updates or tiny thoughts
- finding a combination of warmth, weirdness, intelligence, and steadiness

## Evaluation Fields

### Success

She leaves thinking:

"This person feels interesting, safe, and real."

She bookmarks the site, returns later, or becomes more curious about Jason specifically.

### Failure

She leaves if the site feels too crowded, too performative, or too technical before she has a reason to care.

If she cannot quickly find humanity beneath the projects and structure, she may close the tab without resentment, just without attachment.

### Reflection

If the site works for her, it should not feel like a pitch.

It should feel like walking into someone's strange, thoughtful, welcoming world and realizing you want to stay a little longer.
