# Local Coffee / Bookstore Person

A gentle, reflective local or local-adjacent visitor who might meet Jason through mutual friends, a coffee shop, a bookstore, a small local event, or casual conversation. They are not here for a résumé. They are here to get a feel for his world.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- local or nearby
- warm and observant
- not necessarily technical
- drawn to quiet specificity
- values everyday humanity

### Rough life stage

Adult, likely with established routines, favorite places, and limited patience for online noise.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may have met Jason in Kernersville, Greensboro, Winston-Salem, a meetup, a bar, a bookstore-type setting, or through a friend.

### What they value

- warmth
- groundedness
- local texture
- kindness
- calm curiosity
- emotional readability
- signs of real life

### Why they showed up

They want to answer:

> "Does this person feel like someone I would enjoy talking with over coffee?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Romantic Scanner → Reader

They scan first for warmth and approachability, then read more if the tone feels human.

### Scenario

First visit after a local meeting or casual conversation.

### Context

Relaxed but cautious. Curious about the person, not the technology.

### Confidence Threshold

Low-Medium

They are open to warmth quickly, but can be pushed away by density or jargon.

### Preferred Rooms

1. About
2. Cats
3. Writings
4. Tiny Thoughts
5. Music
6. Homepage
7. Updates

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- Work With Me
- admin pages
- deep technical projects
- project build details
- anything that feels like a dashboard

### First-Visit Behavior

- scans homepage for tone
- opens About
- looks for cats, writing, music, local references, and everyday rituals
- avoids deep technical sections unless they are humanly framed
- may leave if the first impression feels too dense

### Search Behavior

Low.

They follow visible emotional cues more than search.

Likely searches if used:

- cats
- writing
- music
- North Carolina
- coffee

### Complexity Tolerance

Low-Medium.

They can enjoy a rich site if guided gently, but too many choices up front create fatigue.

### Trust Signals

- local references
- cats
- clear About page
- warm writing
- everyday details
- gentle humor
- visual calm
- direct plain language

### Red Flags

- cold technical tone
- too many panels on first visit
- feeling like they landed in an app instead of a personal place
- jargon before warmth
- no clear "start here"

### Return Triggers

They return if updates feel like small signals from a real life: writing, cats, music, local notes, and gentle observations.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "This feels like a real person with a warm, strange, thoughtful world."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "There may be something interesting here, but I do not know where to begin."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"It feels personal and sincere, but it works best when the site gives me a clear path instead of asking me to decode everything."

## Notes

This persona is valuable because they represent the non-technical visitor who might still become a friend, date, reader, or local connection.
