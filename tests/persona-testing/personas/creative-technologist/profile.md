# Creative Technologist

A designer-developer, systems thinker, creative coder, or digital craft person who lands on ArcadeGhosts because the phrase "personal site with rooms" sounds promising. They care about implementation, but they care just as much about taste, framing, coherence, and whether the site earns its own strangeness.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- designer-developer
- creative technologist
- digital artist
- systems-minded writer or builder
- someone who likes tools with personality

### Rough life stage

Likely adult, professionally experienced, and used to seeing many portfolios, side projects, and experimental personal sites.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may arrive through GitHub, a shared AI/design conversation, a business card, a social post, or a recommendation from someone who says, "This site is weird in a good way."

### What they value

- taste
- coherence
- craft
- intentionality
- editorial judgment
- technical competence
- original framing
- systems that feel authored

### Why they showed up

They want to know whether ArcadeGhosts is merely aesthetic, or whether the structure, writing, tools, and experiments are all pulling in the same direction.

Their core question:

> "Is this just a quirky personal site, or is there real design and systems thinking underneath it?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Wanderer → Builder

They begin by exploring broadly, then focus on projects, build logs, tooling, and anything that reveals how the system works.

### Scenario

First visit after hearing about ArcadeGhosts as an unusually personal software/design experiment.

### Context

Curious, visually alert, moderately patient, and looking for evidence that the concept has substance.

### Confidence Threshold

Medium

They are open to being impressed, but they need the execution to support the concept.

### Preferred Rooms

1. Homepage
2. Build Log
3. Projects
4. Search
5. Music
6. Writings
7. Work With Me
8. Twin Peaks Self

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- admin pages unless they are framed as part of the tooling story
- purely nostalgic pages without design/system context
- pages that feel like static lists rather than authored rooms

### First-Visit Behavior

- scans the homepage for concept clarity
- clicks into Build Log or Projects quickly
- checks whether the "rooms" metaphor is real or decorative
- notices typography, density, visual rhythm, and navigation
- may search for "AI", "projects", "build", or "tools"
- reads one or two pages deeply if the design feels intentional

### Search Behavior

Medium.

They use search if navigation feels dense or if they want to test whether the site has a real information architecture.

Likely searches:

- AI
- build log
- projects
- design
- tools
- ArcadeGhosts

### Complexity Tolerance

High, if complexity feels intentional.

Low, if it feels accidental.

### Trust Signals

- visible build history
- strong design language
- coherent cross-linking
- clear explanations of why features exist
- unusual ideas grounded by working implementation
- small details that feel authored rather than generated

### Red Flags

- novelty without utility
- too many panels fighting for attention
- inconsistent tone between rooms
- generic AI language
- pages that look cool but do not reward interaction
- public-facing admin paths without enough context

### Return Triggers

They return if the site feels alive, if new experiments appear, or if the build log makes them feel like they are watching a distinctive system evolve.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "This is not just a portfolio. This is a designed personal operating system with a real point of view."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "There are interesting ideas here, but the site is more concept than craft."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"ArcadeGhosts has more personality than most personal sites, and the best parts are where the design, writing, and tooling all reinforce each other."

## Notes

The Creative Technologist is useful because they test whether ArcadeGhosts is coherent as a designed system, not just emotionally resonant or technically functional.
