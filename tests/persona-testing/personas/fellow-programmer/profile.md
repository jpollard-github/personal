# Fellow Programmer

A technical peer who finds ArcadeGhosts through GitHub, AI tooling, a side-project conversation, or shared interest in weird software. They do not need the site to be corporate, but they do need evidence that the craft, implementation, and thinking are real.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- experienced engineer
- technical lead
- independent builder
- AI tooling enthusiast
- architecture-minded developer
- someone skeptical of vague claims

### Rough life stage

Likely professionally experienced enough to recognize real work and dismiss shallow self-branding quickly.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may discover Jason through dev-assistant, ArcadeGhosts, an AI meetup, GitHub, LinkedIn, or a technical conversation.

### What they value

- evidence
- architecture
- practical tradeoffs
- clear implementation thinking
- real shipping
- humility about limitations
- technical taste

### Why they showed up

They want to answer:

> "Is this person actually building interesting things, or just talking about building things?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Builder → Scanner

They look for projects, architecture, build logs, and proof quickly. If those are strong, they slow down.

### Scenario

Evaluating whether Jason is technically interesting enough to follow, collaborate with, hire, or talk shop with.

### Context

Curious but skeptical. Comfortable with complexity. Low patience for fluff.

### Confidence Threshold

Medium

Technical proof builds trust faster than aesthetic polish.

### Preferred Rooms

1. Projects
2. Build Log
3. Work With Me
4. Search
5. About
6. Updates
7. Music or other generated systems if framed technically

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- cat pages
- dating/personality-adjacent writing
- purely nostalgic pages
- fan homage pages without technical framing

### First-Visit Behavior

- scans homepage for project links
- opens Build Log or Projects early
- looks for signs of actual implementation
- may search for "AI", "MCP", "Vercel", "Next.js", "tooling", or "generator"
- checks whether personal style is backed by working systems

### Search Behavior

High.

Likely searches:

- AI
- dev assistant
- MCP
- generator
- Vercel
- projects
- architecture

### Complexity Tolerance

High.

They can handle dense pages if headings and structure are strong.

### Trust Signals

- specific build-log entries
- concrete project descriptions
- visible constraints and tradeoffs
- generated artifacts
- working tools
- source or implementation notes
- realistic AI claims

### Red Flags

- vague AI hype
- generic project cards
- too much aesthetic language without implementation
- no clear path to technical details
- impressive claims without artifacts

### Return Triggers

They return if projects evolve, build logs stay active, or a tool like persona testing or brand generation becomes technically interesting.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "This person has taste, ships things, and thinks about systems in a way I respect."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "Interesting personality, but I cannot tell whether the technical work is substantial."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"The personal tone is unusual for a developer site, but the build log and generated systems make it clear there is real engineering underneath."

## Notes

This persona is a useful stress test for whether ArcadeGhosts can remain personal without losing technical credibility.
