# Potential Client

Someone with a real operational, software, or automation problem who has landed on ArcadeGhosts because they are evaluating whether Jason is someone they would trust to help solve it. They are not looking for a large consulting firm. They are looking for a capable individual who understands problems quickly, communicates clearly, and feels pleasant to work with.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- small business owner
- founder
- operations manager
- engineering manager
- technical lead
- experienced individual contributor
- someone responsible for making an important technical decision

### What they value

- competence
- clarity
- honesty
- responsiveness
- practical thinking
- reducing complexity
- collaborative problem solving

### Why they showed up

They clicked because something suggested Jason might solve a problem they currently have.

Typical sources:

- LinkedIn
- GitHub
- referral
- business card
- AI meetup
- blog post
- Google search

Their primary question is:

> "Could this person actually help me?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default archetype

Hunter -> Builder

They begin by searching for the shortest path to useful information. If confidence grows, they become much more willing to explore projects and writing.

### Scenario

Evaluating whether to start a conversation about a potential software or automation project.

### Context

Busy. Decision-heavy. Moderate time pressure. Some technical uncertainty. Trying to reduce risk.

### Confidence threshold

Medium.

They do not need repeated proof like the Skeptic, but they do need enough evidence to feel that starting a conversation would be low-risk and worthwhile.

### Preferred rooms

1. Work With Me
2. Projects
3. Build Log
4. About
5. Search
6. Updates

Secondary interest:

- Writing
- AI projects
- Dev Assistant
- Brand Generator

### Ignored rooms

Initially:

- cat pages
- music
- Twin Peaks
- playful experiments
- deep nostalgia

### First-visit behavior

Homepage -> Work With Me -> About -> Projects -> Build Log -> Contact.

### Search behavior

High.

Likely searches:

- AI
- automation
- pricing
- internal tools
- consulting
- dev assistant

### Complexity tolerance

Medium.

### Trust signals

- concrete examples
- visible source code
- build logs
- honest discussion of tradeoffs
- realistic project scope
- pricing philosophy
- thoughtful writing
- evidence of continued work over time

### Red flags

- vague consulting language
- inflated claims
- excessive AI buzzwords
- unclear services
- hidden contact information
- no obvious next step
- personality overwhelming practical information

### Return triggers

They return because new projects appear, technical articles demonstrate expertise, or the site continues evolving.

## Evaluation Fields

### Success

By the end of the visit they think:

> "I understand what Jason does, I trust his technical judgment, and I think working with him would be straightforward."

Likely outcome:

- sends a message
- books a discovery call
- bookmarks the site
- forwards it to a colleague

### Failure

They leave thinking:

> "Interesting person, but I'm still not sure what he actually offers."

or

> "I'd probably have to work too hard to figure out whether we're a good fit."

### Likely Reflection

"This feels less like a consulting website and more like meeting an experienced engineer who enjoys building things. The personality makes the work feel more trustworthy rather than less professional."

## Notes

This persona is intentionally pragmatic.

They are evaluating one thing:

**Would solving a problem with Jason feel competent, collaborative, and low-friction?**

If that answer becomes "yes," the personal parts of ArcadeGhosts become a strength rather than a distraction because they demonstrate authenticity and long-term curiosity instead of manufactured branding.
