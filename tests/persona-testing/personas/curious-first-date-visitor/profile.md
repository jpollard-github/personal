# Curious First-Date Visitor

A thoughtful, emotionally aware person who found ArcadeGhosts because she is curious about Jason as a person. She is not evaluating every project or reading like a client. She is trying to sense whether the person behind the site feels warm, real, interesting, emotionally safe, and worth meeting.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- emotionally observant
- cautious but curious
- likely in a similar life stage
- interested in personality before résumé
- open to unusual people if they feel grounded

### Rough life stage

Likely midlife or close enough to understand second chapters, complexity, grief, work history, and the desire for a calmer but still interesting life.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

She may arrive from a dating profile, a conversation, a business card, a shared link, or a quiet curiosity after meeting Jason in person.

### What she values

- warmth
- curiosity
- emotional safety
- humor
- self-awareness
- consistency
- kindness
- signs of real life

### Why she showed up

She wants to answer:

> "Would I feel safe and interested spending time with this person?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Romantic Reader → Scanner

She begins by looking for personality and warmth, then reads more deeply if the site feels safe.

### Scenario

First visit before deciding whether to continue a conversation, meet for coffee, or reply with more curiosity.

### Context

Evening browsing. Emotionally open but cautious. Enough time to explore, but not enough patience for a maze.

### Confidence Threshold

Medium

She wants to be interested, but trust must form through tone and consistency.

### Preferred Rooms

1. About
2. Writings
3. Cats
4. Music
5. Tiny Thoughts
6. Twin Peaks Self
7. Homepage

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- Work With Me
- admin pages
- detailed technical project pages
- pricing or consulting material
- deeply technical build-log entries

These may become interesting later if the human connection forms first.

### First-Visit Behavior

- scans homepage for emotional tone
- opens About early
- reads cat and writing pages
- checks whether interests feel alive rather than performative
- may skim Music or Tiny Thoughts
- notices whether the site feels warm or overwhelming

### Search Behavior

Low to Medium.

She is more likely to follow emotional cues than use search immediately.

Likely searches if she does use it:

- cats
- Twin Peaks
- writing
- music
- Thomas
- coffee

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium-Low.

She can enjoy richness, but too many choices too early may feel like work.

### Trust Signals

- cats by name
- emotionally honest writing
- self-awareness without oversharing
- humor
- signs of everyday life
- thoughtful About page
- interests explained through feeling, not just lists

### Red Flags

- emotional intensity without grounding
- too much technical material before human context
- chaotic navigation
- dating-adjacent self-presentation that feels performative
- too much unresolved sadness without signs of care or resilience

### Return Triggers

She returns if she feels there is more to learn about Jason, especially through writing, cats, music, and recent updates that feel alive.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

She thinks:

> "This person is unusual, but warm. I want to know more."

### What failure looks like

She thinks:

> "There may be something interesting here, but the site feels too overwhelming or emotionally hard to read."

### What she would likely say after visiting

"The site is a lot, but the best parts made me feel like I was meeting a real person, not a profile."

## Notes

This persona overlaps with Ideal Partner, but she is more situational and early-stage. She is not yet imagining a relationship. She is deciding whether curiosity should continue.
