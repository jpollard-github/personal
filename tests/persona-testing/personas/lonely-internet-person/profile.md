# Lonely Internet Person

A person who is not necessarily looking for software, dating, or services. They are looking for signs of a real human being online. They miss the old internet: strange personal sites, emotionally honest writing, small communities, and places that felt less optimized and more alive.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- emotionally perceptive
- possibly lonely or isolated
- nostalgic for personal internet spaces
- drawn to warmth and specificity
- wary of algorithmic feeds and polished self-branding

### Rough life stage

Any adult life stage, but likely someone who has felt the loss of real online places.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may arrive through a shared link, a search result, a social post, a business card, or random curiosity.

### What they value

- authenticity
- warmth
- emotional resonance
- little details
- non-algorithmic discovery
- signs of ongoing life
- personal writing
- gentle weirdness

### Why they showed up

They want to answer:

> "Is this one of those rare online places that feels real?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Wanderer → Reader

They click widely at first, then settle into emotionally resonant rooms.

### Scenario

First visit while wanting connection, warmth, or a break from empty feeds.

### Context

Late night, emotionally open, somewhat tired, looking for signal.

### Confidence Threshold

Low-Medium

They are ready to connect if the site feels human, but can be hurt by coldness or artificiality.

### Preferred Rooms

1. About
2. Writings
3. Cats
4. Tiny Thoughts
5. Updates
6. Music
7. Guestbook
8. Homepage

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- Work With Me
- admin pages
- pricing
- technical documentation
- formal professional pages

### First-Visit Behavior

- scans for warmth
- follows emotionally titled links
- reads About, cats, writing, or tiny thoughts
- may check Guestbook if it exists
- notices whether the site feels like a place rather than a funnel
- may wander more than most personas

### Search Behavior

Low-Medium.

Likely searches:

- cats
- writing
- lonely
- music
- Thomas
- Twin Peaks
- tiny thoughts

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium.

They can handle many rooms if the experience feels like wandering, not being lost.

### Trust Signals

- specific personal stories
- cats
- grief handled gently
- ordinary life details
- non-salesy tone
- guestbook/community signals
- recent updates
- authorial voice

### Red Flags

- too much self-promotion
- cold technical tone
- no clear human entry point
- obvious AI-generated filler
- overwhelming density without emotional anchor
- feeling like the site wants something from them

### Return Triggers

They return for new writing, Tiny Thoughts, cat updates, Music, Guestbook activity, or the feeling that ArcadeGhosts is still alive.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "This feels like a real person made a place, and I want to come back."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "This has interesting parts, but I did not find the warmth I was hoping for."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"It reminds me of the internet when people made places instead of content."

## Notes

This may be one of the strongest ArcadeGhosts personas. It tests resonance, warmth, returnability, and whether the site feels like a place worth inhabiting.
