# Returning Fan

A repeat visitor who already likes ArcadeGhosts and wants reasons to keep checking back. They know the tone. They are now evaluating freshness, momentum, and whether new work stays easy to find.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- already familiar with the site
- likes at least one major room
- curious about updates
- willing to explore new sections
- more tolerant than first-time visitors

### Rough life stage

Any adult visitor who has already decided ArcadeGhosts is worth occasional attention.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may be a friend, follower, returning reader, technical peer, local contact, or someone who bookmarked the site.

### What they value

- freshness
- momentum
- clear updates
- a sense of ongoing life
- familiar tone with new discoveries
- easy re-entry

### Why they showed up

They want to answer:

> "What's new since last time?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

RSS Subscriber → Wanderer

They want a quick update path first, then may wander if something catches them.

### Scenario

Returning after days or weeks away.

### Context

Moderate attention. Already warm toward the site. Looking for novelty.

### Confidence Threshold

Low

Trust already exists. The site mostly needs to reward return behavior.

### Preferred Rooms

1. Updates
2. Build Log
3. Tiny Thoughts
4. Writings
5. Music
6. Homepage spotlight
7. Projects

### Ignored Rooms

Often:

- static About page
- old archive pages unless resurfaced
- admin pages
- unchanged foundational pages

### First-Visit Behavior

On return, they:

- look for Updates or Recent Changes
- scan dates
- check new Tiny Thoughts
- open a new writing or build-log entry
- may use Search if they remember something specific
- need to avoid re-learning the whole map

### Search Behavior

Medium.

Likely searches:

- recent
- updates
- build log
- music
- writing
- whatever they remember from last time

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium-High.

They already know the site, but stale or hidden updates frustrate them.

### Trust Signals

- visible dates
- recent updates
- RSS feeds
- build-log continuity
- homepage spotlight changes
- "what changed" pages
- clear return paths

### Red Flags

- no obvious new material
- old content dominating homepage
- updates buried too deeply
- repeated framing that slows return visits
- no RSS or notification path

### Return Triggers

They return when the site teaches them that return visits are rewarded.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "I can immediately see what's new, and there is still life here."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "I still like the site, but I cannot tell what changed."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"ArcadeGhosts works best as a living place when the newest signals are easy to spot."

## Notes

This persona is essential for testing update loops, RSS value, homepage freshness, and whether the site can become a habit instead of a one-time curiosity.
