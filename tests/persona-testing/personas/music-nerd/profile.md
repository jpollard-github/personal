# Music Nerd

A serious listener who comes to ArcadeGhosts for music first. They may be interested in playlists, listening history, genre drift, stats, emotional associations, or simply whether Jason's taste has enough specificity to reward exploration.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- serious music listener
- playlist obsessive
- stats-curious listener
- genre wanderer
- someone who uses music as identity, memory, and mood regulation

### Rough life stage

Any adult life stage, but old enough to have eras of listening history and opinions about them.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may arrive through Music League, a shared song, a social post, a playlist, or a card that made ArcadeGhosts sound like a strange listening archive.

### What they value

- taste
- specificity
- curation
- emotional context
- genre movement
- listening history
- surprise
- repeat-visit value

### Why they showed up

They want to answer:

> "Is this music room actually interesting, or just a stats dashboard?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Hunter → Wanderer

They go straight to Music, then wander if the music room suggests deeper connections.

### Scenario

Evaluating the music section as a listening archive and personality signal.

### Context

Curious, opinionated, and willing to explore if the payoff is real.

### Confidence Threshold

Medium

They need the music room to demonstrate taste quickly.

### Preferred Rooms

1. Music
2. Tiny Thoughts
3. Updates
4. About
5. Writings
6. Movies & TV
7. Search

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- Work With Me
- admin pages
- technical project details
- cat rooms unless music links emotionally to them
- business-oriented pages

### First-Visit Behavior

- opens Music quickly
- scans for top artists, recent listening, genre patterns, and unusual commentary
- looks for human interpretation, not just data
- may search for artists, genres, or "playlist"
- follows cross-links if music connects to writing, memories, or current mood

### Search Behavior

Medium-High.

Likely searches:

- music
- playlist
- Spotify
- CHVRCHES
- Twin Peaks
- metal
- synthwave
- Music League

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium-High.

They can tolerate density if the page feels like a real archive.

### Trust Signals

- actual listening data
- clear curation
- strong commentary
- music tied to memory and mood
- recent updates
- not pretending taste is cooler than it is
- honest enthusiasm

### Red Flags

- stats without voice
- generic genre labels
- no obvious "best entry point"
- music content buried behind non-music navigation
- too much visual style without listening payoff

### Return Triggers

They return for new playlists, listening reports, Music League posts, recent favorites, and personal notes about songs.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "This feels like a real listening archive with personality. I want to poke around."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "There are stats here, but I do not feel the taste or the person behind them."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"The music section is strongest when it combines data with personal interpretation. The numbers are cool, but the voice is what makes it worth returning to."

## Notes

This persona should strongly weight `/music` and lightly discount unrelated rooms unless they create useful emotional context.
