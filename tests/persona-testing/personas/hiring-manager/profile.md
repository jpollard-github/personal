# Hiring Manager

A hiring manager, director, or senior technical decision maker who lands on ArcadeGhosts while evaluating Jason as a potential employee, contractor, lead developer, or high-trust technical contributor. They care about personality, but only after they see competence, judgment, and professional fit.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- engineering manager
- director
- startup founder
- technical recruiter with context
- senior developer involved in hiring
- someone evaluating professional risk

### Rough life stage

Professionally experienced and responsible for making or influencing hiring decisions.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may arrive from LinkedIn, a résumé, GitHub, referral, job application, AI meetup, or business card.

### What they value

- competence
- judgment
- consistency
- communication
- evidence of shipping
- technical range
- leadership maturity
- low drama

### Why they showed up

They want to answer:

> "Would I trust this person with important technical work?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Builder → Hunter

They quickly seek professional evidence, then look for specific supporting details.

### Scenario

Evaluating whether Jason should be interviewed, referred, contracted, or trusted with a leadership-level technical problem.

### Context

Time-constrained, evidence-seeking, open to personality if it supports credibility.

### Confidence Threshold

Medium-High

They need several consistent signals before confidence forms.

### Preferred Rooms

1. Work With Me
2. Projects
3. Build Log
4. About
5. Resume
6. Search
7. Updates

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- cats
- music
- Twin Peaks
- arcade nostalgia
- playful experiments without work relevance
- admin pages

### First-Visit Behavior

- scans homepage for professional clarity
- looks for projects and work evidence
- opens Work With Me or résumé-related material
- checks build log for consistency and recent activity
- may search for "AI", "architecture", "leadership", "resume", or "projects"
- samples About to evaluate communication style

### Search Behavior

High.

Likely searches:

- resume
- projects
- AI
- architecture
- leadership
- work
- developer

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium.

They can handle detail if it is organized around decisions and outcomes.

### Trust Signals

- concrete projects
- technical depth
- visible shipping history
- clear communication
- leadership examples
- realistic claims
- evidence of collaboration
- well-maintained site

### Red Flags

- too much personal material before professional clarity
- unclear work history
- vague project descriptions
- AI hype without specifics
- no obvious résumé/contact path
- tone that feels too informal for high-trust work

### Return Triggers

They return if they are considering Jason for a role, want to share the site with a colleague, or need to compare his work against other candidates.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "This person is technically strong, self-directed, thoughtful, and probably unusually productive."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "Interesting person, but I cannot quickly assess professional fit."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"The personal site is unconventional, but the build history and technical range make the candidate memorable."

## Notes

This persona is portable beyond ArcadeGhosts and useful for product demos. It tests whether a personal site can remain expressive while still supporting professional evaluation.
