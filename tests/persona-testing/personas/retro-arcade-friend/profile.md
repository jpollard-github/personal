# Retro Arcade Friend

A playful visitor who loves old cabinets, quarter-light nostalgia, weird media, and the feeling of following one glowing machine to another. They may know games deeply, or they may simply love the aura around arcades.

## Identity Fields

### Who this person is

- arcade fan
- retro-game person
- nostalgia collector
- convention wanderer
- someone who likes fun things taken seriously

### Rough life stage

Likely old enough to have real nostalgia for arcades, but could also be younger and drawn to the aesthetic.

### Relationship to Jason or the site

They may arrive through ArcadeGhosts branding, a business card, a game page, a social post, or a conversation about old cabinets.

### What they value

- atmosphere
- playfulness
- specificity
- memory
- references
- surprise
- a sense that the fun is genuine

### Why they showed up

They want to answer:

> "Does this site actually have arcade soul, or is the name just aesthetic?"

## Behavior Fields

### Default Archetype

Wanderer

They follow curiosity and visual cues more than linear navigation.

### Scenario

First visit after seeing the name ArcadeGhosts or hearing that Jason likes arcades and weird little experiments.

### Context

Playful, open, exploratory.

### Confidence Threshold

Low-Medium

They are easy to engage if the site gives them a fun door quickly.

### Preferred Rooms

1. Arcade
2. Games
3. Between Two Lodges
4. Homepage
5. Music
6. Movies & TV
7. About

### Ignored Rooms

Initially:

- Work With Me
- admin pages
- pricing
- long technical pages unless playful
- formal résumé material

### First-Visit Behavior

- looks for Arcade or games immediately
- clicks visual/fun labels
- explores cabinets, nostalgia rooms, and interactive experiments
- may return to homepage after playful detours
- tolerates weirdness if orientation remains clear

### Search Behavior

Medium.

Likely searches:

- Galaga
- arcade
- games
- Twin Peaks
- Between Two Lodges
- retro

### Complexity Tolerance

Medium.

They can handle visual flourish but dislike dead ends.

### Trust Signals

- real game references
- personal memories
- playful labels
- strong atmosphere
- interactive rooms
- music/movie connections
- "this exists because I love it" energy

### Red Flags

- arcade content feels thin
- too much serious explanation around fun pages
- weak imagery
- no obvious game/arcade path
- nostalgia without personal specificity

### Return Triggers

They return if new games, cabinet notes, weird experiments, or visual rooms appear.

## Evaluation Fields

### What success looks like

They think:

> "This feels like someone who actually loves this stuff."

### What failure looks like

They think:

> "Cool name, but the arcade side feels more like a label than a room."

### What they would likely say after visiting

"The best parts are where the site feels playful without apologizing for it."

## Notes

This persona should reward atmosphere, visual cues, interactive detours, and arcade-specific content.
