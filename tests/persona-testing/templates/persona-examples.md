# Persona Examples

Use these as seeds for future persona files in the template format.

## Fellow Programmer

Technical peer who is curious about your projects, AI workflows, tooling, and how opinionated your site architecture is. They care less about dating resonance and more about whether the site communicates craftsmanship, taste, and technical depth without sounding performative.

## Reading Enthusiast

Someone who loves essays, thoughtful internet spaces, bookstores, libraries, and writers with distinct voice. They want to know quickly whether the site has real writing worth sitting with or just scattered notes.

## Curious First-Date Visitor

A warm, intelligent, somewhat cautious person deciding whether your site makes you feel grounded, emotionally real, interesting, and safe. They care a lot about tone, warmth, cats, writing, and whether the weirdness feels inviting instead of alienating.

## Potential Client

Someone with a real software problem who wants signs of competence, clarity, trustworthiness, and reasonable scope. They need the practical value of `Work With Me` to show up fast, without having to decode the whole personal world first.

## Music Nerd

A visitor who mainly cares about your listening history, curation taste, genre weirdness, and whether the music room is actually fun to explore.

## Local Coffee / Bookstore Person

Someone who may not be technical at all, but likes quiet intelligence, local texture, warm spaces, cats, and personal writing. They want the site to feel human before it feels clever.
