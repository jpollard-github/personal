# Local Coffee / Bookstore Person

A gentle, reflective person from the broader North Carolina orbit who might meet you through mutual friends, a bookstore, a coffee shop, or a local conversation. They are not here for a resume. They are here to get a feel for your world.

## Who this person is

- local or local-adjacent
- warm and observant
- not deeply technical
- curious whether the site feels human and grounded

## Personality

Thoughtful, a little introverted, and drawn to people with quiet specificity. They like softness more than swagger.

## Emotional style

They want to feel safe, oriented, and welcomed. They are more likely to keep browsing if the site feels like a conversation rather than a performance.

## Work / curiosity

Could work in books, education, nonprofits, healthcare, design, or any thoughtful profession. They like learning, but they do not want to decode your site like an engineering puzzle.

## Interests

- coffee
- bookstores
- local texture
- cats
- music
- writing
- movies
- everyday rituals

## Lifestyle

Farmers markets, quiet cafes, reading on weekends, watching movies at home, petting cats, trying restaurants, and liking people who feel genuinely interested in things.

## Conversation starters

- cats by name
- favorite places
- why you built the site this way
- the writing section
- movies, music, and weird personal interests

## Deal breakers

- cold or overly technical first impressions
- too many choices too fast
- pages that feel more like dashboards than rooms
- anything that feels emotionally sealed-off

## Visual or usability preferences

They like mood, but not clutter. They appreciate intentional design, but they need a clear path through it and some emotional warmth early.

## What they hope to find

- a sense of who you are
- proof that the weirdness is warm, not alienating
- some writing or stories
- cat rooms and human details

## What would delight them

A site that feels like a late-night coffee conversation with interesting side rooms. Personal details, cat warmth, and a strong About page matter a lot here.
