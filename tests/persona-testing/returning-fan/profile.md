# Returning Fan

A repeat visitor who already likes ArcadeGhosts and wants reasons to keep checking back. They know the tone. They are now evaluating freshness, discoverability, and whether new work stays easy to notice.

## Who this person is

- already familiar with the site
- likes at least one major room already
- returning for updates, writing, projects, or curiosity

## Personality

Engaged, affectionate toward the site, and willing to explore. They are more tolerant than first-time visitors, but more likely to notice stale patterns.

## Emotional style

They want momentum, freshness, and a sense that the site is alive. They also want it to stay easy to re-enter without re-learning the whole map.

## Work / curiosity

Could be technical or not. Their main question is whether the site still rewards attention over time.

## Interests

- updates
- build log
- new writings
- new projects
- spotlight changes
- new interactive rooms

## Lifestyle

Habitual browsing, checking favorite sites, following creators they like, and appreciating signs that a place is still evolving.

## Conversation starters

- what changed recently
- what is active now
- what room is new
- where to go next if they have been away for a month

## Deal breakers

- stale homepage emphasis
- updates that are hard to find
- old sections dominating current work
- too much repeated framing before getting to what is new

## Visual or usability preferences

They know the site’s style already. Their main preference is for clearer freshness cues and quicker re-entry into new material.

## What they hope to find

- recent work quickly
- strong update loops
- new surprises
- signs of momentum

## What would delight them

A homepage and build-log path that makes the newest meaningful changes easy to spot, plus enough curated variety to remind them why the site is fun.
